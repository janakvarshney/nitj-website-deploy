About PMJVK:
Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS), under Ministry of Minority Affairs (MoMA) is a development programme under which community infrastructure and basic amenities are being created in the identified areas. PMJVK aims to bridge development gaps in identified minority concentration areas by creating community infrastructure and basic amenities. The scheme is primarily implemented through State/UT Governments. Proposals are recommended by State Level Committees (headed by the Chief Secretary) and then approved by the Empowered Committee of PMJVK in the Ministry of Minority Affairs.

While traditional projects under PMJVK include schools, colleges, ITIs, hospitals, etc., the scheme has been restructured and approved with revised guidelines from FY 2022-23 to cover all districts. The emphasis is on education, health, skill development, and women-oriented projects.

The establishment of the Centre of Excellence '5G and Beyond' under the Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK) at Dr. B. R. Ambedkar National Institute of Technology, Jalandhar, marks a pivotal moment in our commitment to inclusive development. This initiative perfectly aligns with the Ministry of Minority Affairs' vision to bridge socio-economic gaps by creating cutting-edge infrastructure and developing advanced technological skills. It will empower minority communities to thrive in the rapidly evolving digital landscape, ensuring they are not just participants but leaders in India's technological advancement and contribute significantly to a digitally inclusive nation.

 

Vision:
To be a leading Centre of Excellence in 5G and emerging technologies, dedicated to empowering minority communities through advanced training, innovation, and fostering inclusive regional development


Mission:
To equip individuals with practical skills and industry-relevant expertise in 5G and emerging technologies through hands-on education, thereby empowering minority communities and bridging the employment gap in underserved regions


Target beneficiaries:
The primary beneficiaries of the 5G and Beyond centre include minority students, unemployed youth, and aspiring entrepreneurs from the region. Educational institutions and industry partners also benefit through collaboration, research, and access to skilled talent.

The 5G Centre of Excellence will offer specialized training programs catering to students, researchers, and industry professionals:
 

  1. Fundamentals & Advanced Concepts
    • 5G Network Architecture, Protocols, and Deployment Strategies.
    • Massive MIMO, Beamforming, and mmWave Communication.
    • Network Slicing and Edge Computing.
    • AI and Machine Learning Applications in 5G.

  2. Hands-on Laboratory Sessions
    • Antenna and RF circuit design using simulation tools.
    • 5G device testing, spectrum analysis, and signal integrity verification.
    • Software-defined radios (SDR) and real-time protocol analysis.

  3. Industry Collaboration & Internships
    • Short-term internships with leading telecom companies and startups.
    • Joint research projects and industrial problem-solving competitions.
    • Live industry case studies on 5G deployment and network optimization.

  4. Certifications & Online Learning
    • Industry-recognized certification programs for 5G engineers.
    • Web-based learning modules with remote-access test labs.

Dr Nitesh Kashyap

Dr. Nitesh Kashyap is an Assistant Professor (Grade-I) in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering. He holds a Ph.D. in Antenna Engineering from PDPM Indian Institute of Information Technology, Design and Manufacturing (IIITDM), Jabalpur. His research expertise lies in advanced antenna design, including EBG-based antennas, beamforming antennas, lens antennas, conformal antennas, metamaterial-based antennas, and Filtennas. Dr. Kashyap is committed to developing innovative antenna solutions for next-generation communication systems and contributes actively to both academic research and technical education in the field.


Dr Neetu Sood

Dr. Neetu Sood earned her Ph.D. from Dr. B R Ambedkar National Institute of Technology, India, after completing her Master’s in Engineering from Thapar University, Patiala. She graduated with a B.E. from SLIET Longowal, where she was awarded a Bronze Medal for her academic excellence. Her Master’s and Ph.D. research focused on Wireless Communication Systems, particularly OFDM technologies. Additionally, she has worked extensively in Biomedical Signal Processing and Plant Signal Processing. Dr. Sood has been a faculty member at NIT Jalandhar since 2007. Under her guidance, four students have completed their Ph.D. in Biomedical Signal Processing, Plant Signalling, and Wireless Sensor Networks, while six more are currently pursuing their research. She has successfully completed a research project funded by MeitY on a women’s safety device and holds two granted patents. Dr. Sood is also the co-founder of INS Technology Private Limited, incubated under NIT Jalandhar.


Dr Aijaz Mehdi Zaidi

Dr. Aijaz Mehdi Zaidi is currently serving as an Assistant Professor at Dr. B R Ambedkar National Institute of Technology, Jalandhar. He holds an M.Tech from IIIT Delhi and a Ph.D. from Jamia Millia Islamia, Delhi, and has previously worked as a Postdoctoral Fellow at IIT Roorkee. His research is primarily focused on the design and development of active and passive RF circuits for advanced wireless communication systems. Dr. Zaidi has contributed to over 25 publications in esteemed international journals, conferences, and book chapters, and has several patents to his name. He is presently heading a research project funded by the Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB), Government of India.


Dr Kundan Kumar

Dr. Kundan Kumar is currently serving as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at Dr. B R Ambedkar National Institute of Technology, Jalandhar. Prior to this, he held academic positions at PEC Chandigarh and SLIET Longowal. He completed his M.Tech and Ph.D. in Electronics Engineering from IIT (ISM) Dhanbad in 2015 and 2020, respectively. Dr. Kumar has qualified the GATE examination seven times in Electronics and Communication Engineering, achieving a top 0.76% rank in 2013. He also cleared the UGC-NET in Electronic Science twice, in 2014. His contributions to research have been recognized through the Research Publication Award from IIT Dhanbad (2017) and a Research Project Award from SLIET Longowal (2023). He has authored over 20 research papers in reputed international journals and conferences, including IEEE publications, and regularly reviews for top-tier journals. His research interests include 5G antennas and circuits, RF/millimeter-wave antennas, cognitive radio, substrate-integrated waveguide-based designs, and RF/microwave sensors.
